My financial diary. Tries to write something everday about the world.

2019/10/30
Big Four audit and accounting firms in the U.K. are selling less nonaudit work to their audit clients. It reflects some regulation parties concern that this kind of non-audit service will compromise indepence of auditors. Personally I’ve seen some mixed evidence on that, because on the other way, non-audit service could help auditors know better about their clients thus provide better auditing service. However, up to now, we don’t have a good measurement of auditing quality, so we cannot tell.

Since 2003, we can see a steady decline in the proportion of non-audit service fee out of total fee income.(Form 25% in 2003 to 8.5% in 2018).

U.K. regulators, including the Competition and Markets Authority, in recent months have proposed an operational split between accounting firms’ audit and consulting business.
